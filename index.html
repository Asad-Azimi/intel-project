Intel’s Sustainability Journey

From early semiconductor innovation to ambitious environmental goals, Intel’s story shows how technology companies can use scale, design, and responsible operations to reduce their impact.

1968

Intel Founded

Intel begins its journey as a semiconductor company.

This milestone represents the beginning of Intel’s role in shaping modern computing and chip manufacturing.

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel introduces a major innovation in computing.

Microprocessors helped make computing smaller, faster, and more accessible, changing how people use technology.

1995

Sustainability Goals Begin

Intel begins setting long-term environmental goals.

Intel’s sustainability work includes reducing emissions, managing water responsibly, minimizing waste, and improving energy use.

2000s

Water Conservation

Intel invests in water recycling and conservation.

Semiconductor manufacturing requires careful resource management, so water conservation is an important part of Intel’s sustainability work.

2020

RISE Strategy Expands

Sustainability expands beyond manufacturing.

Intel’s approach grows to include business units, product life cycles, energy efficiency, and broader environmental impact.

2022

Net-Zero Commitment

Intel commits to net-zero operations emissions by 2040.

This goal focuses on reducing Scope 1 and Scope 2 greenhouse gas emissions across Intel’s global operations.

2030

2030 Sustainability Targets

Intel aims for major progress in energy, water, and waste.

Intel’s 2030 targets include renewable electricity, net-positive water, and zero waste to landfill.

2040

Net-Zero Future

Intel works toward net-zero greenhouse gas emissions.

This future milestone represents Intel’s long-term plan to lower the environmental footprint of its global operations.
